# Pitch Email — California Center of Longevity Medicine

## Step 1 — Capture two screenshots (≈ 60 seconds)

You'll need two PNGs that match in width so the side-by-side feels intentional.

**Screenshot A — current site (the "before"):**
1. Open https://californiacenteroflongevitymedicine.com in Chrome
2. Make the window 1440px wide (or close — full-width on a laptop is fine)
3. Cmd + Shift + 4, then press Space, then click the Chrome window. macOS captures the whole tab content including the URL bar.
4. Save as `current.png`

**Screenshot B — your concept (the "after"):**
1. Open `longevity-homepage.html` in Chrome (double-click it from Finder)
2. Same window width as A
3. Same Cmd + Shift + 4 → Space → click capture
4. Save as `concept.png`

Tip: capture only the **hero region** of each (top of the page through "Live Longer. Live Better." for yours, top through the running-man image for theirs). Long full-page screenshots compress badly in email and the comparison loses impact.

## Step 2 — Build the side-by-side

Easiest path: drag both into Keynote or Google Slides, place side by side at the same height, add small "Current" / "Concept" labels underneath in 12pt gray. Export as PNG.

Even faster: in macOS Preview, open both, select all in one, copy, paste into the other side. Or use a free tool like [Photopea.com](https://photopea.com) if you want pixel control.

Don't bother with elaborate annotations. The visual contrast does the work.

## Step 3 — The email

**Subject line options** (pick one — A is the strongest):

- A. "A redesigned homepage for California Center of Longevity Medicine"
- B. "Concept: a more modern homepage for the practice"
- C. "Quick redesign concept for your website"

**Body:**

---

Hi Dr. Kurtz,

I came across the California Center of Longevity Medicine and was struck by the depth of the practice — three decades of clinical work, functional and longevity training, real physician-led care. The credibility is all there.

The website doesn't quite match it yet. Patients searching for premium longevity care today are landing on practices like Next Health and Parsley Health and forming a first impression in about two seconds.

I put together a quick concept of what a more modern homepage could look like for the practice — same content and credentials, presented in a way that signals the level of care you actually deliver.

[ INSERT side-by-side image here — paste inline, do not attach ]

Happy to share the full scrolling concept if you'd like to see more, or to talk through where this could go from here.

Best,
[Your name]

---

## Why this email works

- **Opens with what's good about them, not what's wrong.** Doctors with 30+ years of credibility don't respond to "your website is bad." They respond to "your website is *underselling* you."
- **Names a specific category gap** — Next Health, Parsley Health — so they know you've actually looked at their market, not blasted a template.
- **Image does the persuasion.** The body just frames it. Three short paragraphs.
- **Soft second-step ask.** "Happy to share the full concept if you'd like to see more" is an invitation, not a closer. Replies feel like requests, not commitments.
- **No link in v1.** The full mockup goes in the *reply* email after they bite. Holding it back actually increases reply rate.

## What to avoid

- Don't write "BEFORE" / "AFTER" in caps on the comparison. It reads infomercial.
- Don't list every section of the redesign in the body. The image already shows it.
- Don't pitch a price or timeline in this email. The goal is just "yes, tell me more."
- Don't attach a PDF deck. PDFs go unopened. Inline image only.
